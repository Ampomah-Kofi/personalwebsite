Research Projects

My research addresses critical challenges in AI trustworthiness, privacy, and security — with a particular focus on real-world social and medical systems.

BotLens — Privacy Auditing for Third-Party Community Bots

BotLens is a privacy auditing framework that systematically analyzes how third-party community bots on social platforms collect, process, and expose user data. As bots become deeply integrated into online communities (Discord, Reddit, Slack), users often have little visibility into what data these bots access. BotLens surfaces these hidden privacy risks and informs better platform governance.

Key contributions:

  • Automated auditing pipeline for bot data-collection behavior
  • Analysis of permission scopes and data access patterns
  • Frameworks for communicating privacy risks to non-expert users

Topics: Usable Privacy · Bot Ecosystems · Security Auditing · Social Platforms

Adversarial Transferability in Medical Imaging Models

This project investigates adversarial transferability — the capacity for adversarial examples crafted against one model to deceive a different model — within medical imaging AI. With AI increasingly used for diagnosis, understanding cross-model vulnerabilities is critical for patient safety and trustworthy deployment.

Key contributions:

  • Multi-metric evaluation framework beyond simple attack success rate
  • Analysis of transferability patterns across model architectures
  • Implications for robustness standards in medical AI systems

Topics: Adversarial Machine Learning · Medical AI · Trustworthy AI · Robustness
